Vibecrate

Music allows us to utilize its power in various ways to fill the needs of our inner strengths, weaknesses, and emotions that certain songs can counter or enable us to release with our present mindset. With the rapid development of technology, music can now be accessible just about anywhere to aid listeners wherever life takes them. Vibecrate is here to help capture the full experience of what music is becoming today without the hassle of multiple sources and limited features. We allow you fulfill the entire experience in the palm of your hands.

Roles UX Design & Research, Visual Design, Brand & Identity

Design Deliverables Competitive Analysis, User Surveys, User Stories & Flows, Personas, Sketching, Prototyping, Wireframing, User Testing, Visual Design

Tools Figma, InVision, UsabilityHub, Axure RP

Problem

Most music applications function identically except a feature or two that really separates them from each other to make them unique. However, this leads to users using three different sources essentially doing the same thing in order to just to benefit from their unique components. At Vibecrate, we want to gather all the elements into one origin for their one stop music source.

Solution

The goal of Vibecrate is to essentially have every feature available to the user without having to navigate through various sources for specific functions. By doing this, we give the user the ability to read through lyrics while a song is playing, establish their own social media page for following artists or kick starting their music career, and to be able to upload their own created music through the cloud.

User Research

  • 64.7% use Spotify as their primary source of music
  • 82.4% use their iPhone as a primary device for listening to music
  • 76.5% say they would like to have lyrics available for their music
  • 75% say their monthly subscription is too expensive
  • 64.7% say they would like to have a social media presence in their applications
  • 100% of users who do not user music applications say all music apps are too identical
  • 10 out of 14 users who use music applications claim the least favorite thing about music applications are the limited use of features if they are not paying for subscription
  • 12 out of 14 of users who use music applications say that their favorite thing is the ability to access their music from anywhere

Competitive Analysis

While the majority of the users use Spotify, Pandora and SoundCloud were the two applications right behind it. What each company lacks is clearly different but they all focus on specific aspects of their application. Pandora is for radio, Soundcloud is for upcoming new artists, and Spotify almost gets to the point of becoming Pandora and Soundcloud combined except it still lacks many features that gives users a reason to not really subscribe for Spotify.

Brand Story

Vibecrate was created based on the past and present world of what music was and how it has evolved to now. Many users are using their mobile devices for music these days but we also have vinyl records and cassette tapes making a huge comeback nearly forty years later despite technological advances. Although my generation has never experience the origin of the vinyl record era, we get a taste of what music was like back when our parents were growing up when they actually had to purchase music at the store.

Mindmapping

Since the decline of that journey has become almost obsolete in today’s society, we can at least experience a hard copy of music using an actual record player to listen to music like it was back in the day. The logo represents the past and present colliding together because music is never outdated regardless of how old it is, we are still storing music somewhere whether it's in the cloud or a milk crate, and we are still vibing out like music has always been. Thus, Vibecrate was born.

Sketches

The logo was in my head once I knew where I was going with this application. I had imagine just a milkcrate with a bunch of records inside of it with the name ‘Vibecrate’ on the of the logos to represent the company name. Sounds easy but after a request to submit more sketch variations, I wanted the records to be next to the crate as well as it would like back in the days when people would store their music. But that was not enough and the logo looked too plain. I wanted to logo to be more lively and eye catching rather than settling for minimal.

Logo Develpoment

After brainstorming and exploring different ways I can create with the logo, I finally settled on what appeared to be the perfect logo design for this project. I had my milk crate, I had the vinyl records, and I also had the vinyl itself with the company name on one of the records. Early stages looked more like an icon so I enhanced a 3D like upgrade to make the logo come to life more.

Protoype

This is the original prototype before extensive design was applied to the application. I had assigned users do the following three tasks:

  1. Create an account and receive feedback on the difficulty for the process to sign up
  2. Upload a song without any assistance from myself
  3. Find the lyrics option while the song is playing to reveal the song’s lyrics

The results turned out to be a success as both users were able to navigate through the tasks with ease. The second user took a little more time finding the feature to upload a song than the first user but eventually discovered the pathway quickly.

Conclusion

The greatest lesson I have learned working on this project is to gain as much information from testing. People see things that you don’t notice and that can impact the project’s route dramatically as it did for me. In the beginning of the testing process, I only observed to see if others would understand my design and although most testers were able to understand the project’s features and navigation, I wish I would have asked more questions and pick the tester’s brains. Challenging them in a way to where their imagination and viewpoint is exposed. Without this tactic in the second testing process, I don’t think this project would have been successful as I thought it would be.

Also, many features that were not included in the start of this project often came later while designing or testing. I believe starting out simple is the best way and the rest will take care of itself as you proceed along the project. You will start to see what works, what doesn’t, and also explore what could it be all at the same time. If there was anything that I could change, it would be the survey’s. I felt I didn’t ask enough questions early on. Thankfully I was able to rebuttal this mistake by asking them in the testing phase.

Final Thoughts

Overall, the project came out to be exactly what I wanted to be. Many things were added on later and also many were removed. But revision and analyzation is what I got out of this experience the most. Testing not only from another person’s perspective but also testing out everything yourself as many times as possible. I did not think that I would end up changing a lot in the end but I started to learn why some of the decisions I made early on were not very great and that was completely fine. Trial and error was greatly present during this project and I had a lot of fun discovering new strengths and weaknesses to improve on along the way.
